The brief

Asset-rich, cash-poor.

Long-term crypto holders face an ugly choice when they need liquidity: sell — triggering taxes and exiting the position — or borrow from a centralized lender at 13–23% APR, handing over custody of their coins to do it.

Starta's bet was that on-chain credit could beat both, if the experience stopped looking like a protocol and started looking like a consumer bank. Collateral stays in self-custodial smart contracts, rates start at 2.47%, and the entire loan — from deposit to draw — lives in a mobile app a first-time DeFi user can operate without a glossary.

As co-founder and CTO, I owned the whole technical surface: the product design language, the mobile app, the lending contracts, and the wallet infrastructure that stitches them together.

The loan flow

Deposit to USDC in seven screens.

Every frame below is rendered live in HTML and CSS — code, not screenshots. The flow compresses a collateralized on-chain loan into the gestures of a consumer banking app: pick collateral, set an amount, see your risk as a price, sign once.

Design decisions

Risk you can read at a glance.

DECISION — RISK

Liquidation is a price, not a ratio.

DeFi quotes risk as LTV percentages. Starta translates it into the one number a holder already thinks in: the BTC price at which liquidation happens. It's computed live as the amount slider moves, and repeated at confirmation and in the open position — always in dollars, always against the current price.

DECISION — TRUST

The full cost, before the signature.

The confirmation sheet itemizes everything the contract will do: existing balance, new principal, the flat 1% origination fee, the due date, and the total — before the wallet is ever asked to sign. Nothing about the loan is discovered after commitment.

DECISION — CLARITY

One number leads every screen.

The dashboard leads with available credit — the answer to "what can I do right now?" — with the balance sheet folded into a single donut. Each deposit row shows the credit it unlocks, not just its balance, so assets read as borrowing power.

Under the hood

The app is a window onto the chain.

There is no private ledger. Every balance, rate, and liquidation price on screen is contract state, read live — the product's honesty is architectural, not editorial.

Protocol

Non-custodial lending contracts

Collateral is escrowed in audited EVM smart contracts. Draws, interest accrual, and liquidations execute on-chain — Starta never takes custody of user assets.

Settlement

USDC loans

Borrowers draw a fully-reserved dollar stablecoin, spendable or off-rampable immediately — no volatile debt denominated in the collateral asset.

Pricing

Oracle-fed liquidations

Collateral is marked to market via decentralized price oracles, driving live liquidation prices in the UI and fair, transparent liquidation triggers in the protocol.

Onboarding

Embedded wallets via Dynamic

Wallet creation and message signing are embedded in the app, so a first loan needs one signature — not a seed-phrase ceremony.

Risk

Real-time position alerts

Configurable notifications fire as collateral prices approach liquidation thresholds, so a margin call is never a surprise.

Flexibility

Pay-as-you-like interest

Interest can be paid down or left to accrue against the position — no monthly payment schedule, no prepayment penalty.
